Welcome to my rambling.

For one of my journalism classes, my instructor encouraged his students to keep journals of their creative process through the semester.

Here's a look at some of the work I did for class, some of what I did outside of class, and I thought through each.

Alphabet project

For this assignment, I had to look for the alphabet in unlikely places.

Sounds easy enough, right?

Well, I had to complicate it. I began looking for the alphabet where nothing existed at all.

I tried to find it in shadows and negative spaces. I wanted to illustrate exactly how much we don't see. By making something that isn't tangible into something meaningful, I hoped to validate the idea that nothing is, in and of itself, something.

That was really, really tricky.

Some were fails. Some were awesome. I really liked the "n" and "g".

A new one for me

This semester, I had to create a WPAP image of myself.

WPAP stands for Wedha's Pop Art Portrait. It uses geometry and repetition, typically along with a popping color pallet, to illustrate a person's face.

It was a really fun piece to produce. It's not often that I get to illustrate, and I appreciated the opportunity.

I wish I would have taken more chances with it, though.

If I were to do it again, I would develop a shape pallet, for lack of a better word. I would choose a shape and repeat it throughout.

I would also diversify my color pallet. I chose these colors because I most identify with them, as is evident on my website and even my resume. But ultimately, it was very limiting.

Designing an advertisement

For this assignment, I had to direct a photo shoot for a White House Black Market advertisement.

It wasn't my first shoot, so I decided to have a little fun.

I put on some crazy music and danced with my model and photographer for a while.

My model had been tense, and having some fun helped her relax enough to stop feeling so self-conscious.

I love the final outcome. But I wish I would have chosen different type and made the clothes more the emphasis than the mood.

I think this would be a better JCPenny ad than WHBM.

I made this piece after seeing some really incredible work from Vox Media. They have remarkable and unconventional ad spots that I think are far more enticing than standard positions.

I wanted to get BallStateDaily.com up to par.

The ad at once commands attention and falls away. It upstages the content without being flashy. Instead of using awkward and forced animation to grab attention, it latches onto a natural user instinct: the scroll.

It almost gives the user control over the ad.

I also see amazing editorial potential for something like this.

Though I don't know exactly what story might lend itself to this sort of tool, I am actively seeking the opportunity to implement it.





Knights of the Dinner Table

This was a super fun project for BallStateDaily.com

Features reporter Danielle Grady wrote this piece about an alumnus who creates "Dungeons and Dragons"-themed comics based on his time at Ball State.

When I read it, I immediately wanted to see the artist's work. But I didn't know how to make random comics break up the story's cohesion.

So, I picked out a few scenes from the story and asked the artist if he would mind helping out. He said yes.

So, we ended up with this awesome piece.

I did all the coding myself, including the responsiveness.
