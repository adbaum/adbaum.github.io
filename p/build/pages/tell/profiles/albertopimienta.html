Alberto Pimienta

Alberto Pimienta is a TV Correspondent for the Latin America Division of Voice of America. He covers everything from the White House, Congress and State Department to U.S. and international breaking news. Voice of America reaches 123 million people every week through radio, television and the web.

Pimienta graduated from Ball State University in Muncie, Indiana with a degree in Journalism and News Broadcasting. While at Ball State, he was reporter and producer for NewsLink, an award-winning live newscast. He also was on staff with the student-run radio station WCRD 91.3 FM, Connections Live, an Emmy Award-winning program on WIPB-TV (Indianapolis DMA) and SportsLink, one of the top sports immersive learning programs in the nation. During college, he also interned at WXIN-TV in Indianapolis, Indiana and the Latin America Division of Voice of America.

Pimienta was born and raised in Maracaibo, Venezuela and moved to the U.S. when he was 17 years old. He reads, writes and speaks Spanish, English and French fluently.
