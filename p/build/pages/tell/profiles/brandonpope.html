Brandon Pope

Brandon Pope is a social media savvy journalist from the Dayton, Ohio area. He is currently a reporter at ABC 57 News in South Bend, Indiana. As a broadcast reporter, he has covered the 2012 Presidential Election, the London Olympics, the Indy 500, college football and the NFL and NBA playoffs.

Pope attended Richmond High School in Richmond, Indiana where he developed a love for broadcasting, earning awards for documentaries and sports play-by-play. He is a proud Ball State University alum and a passionate Ohio sports fan.

He is also a member of the National Association of Black Journalists, where he serves on the National Media Monitoring Committee.

When he isn't bringing you breaking news in the mornings, he enjoys hanging with friends, catching up on long form reads and Netflix shows, and listening to old school jams.

What do advice do you have for aspiring young storytellers?

Question:What made you want to become a journalist?

Answer:I grew up watching a lot of Ohio TV stations and a lot of documentaries. The most intriguing part about those were the way they would take a person and tell their story in such a creative way – in a way that really captured the viewer.

And so it seemed like something you could really have fun with. You get to go to all these cool places, do these cool things. And that’s really what drove me to try it out to see what it was about.

Telling stories, digging deeper, getting a behind-the-scenes look – kind of the exclusivity of it too. You get to see things no one else gets to see. … It’s a great field, and a great thing to do. And I’m passionate about it!

Question:What’s so powerful about telling stories on video?

Answer:Every medium has its strengths and its weaknesses. However, when you look at video, there’s so much to take out of it. It’s one thing to write that somebody was sad or that someone is heartbroken from something. It’s one thing to hear the anguish and grief from them. But when you can see a person’s face; when you can see the tears and you can see the pain and their reaction to something, that communicates something completely different. And that emotion really transcends the screen.

I think that’s something that video does a really great job of. I think it captures emotion better than any other medium. And I think emotion is what connects people to the story and makes people want to read the story and engage in the content.
