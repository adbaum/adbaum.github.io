How do build a multimedia story?

Now that you have your story outlined and your multimedia plan, it’s time to actually build the multimedia components and combine them in a presentation that makes it easy for your readers to interact with. Of course this can also be a complex process.

Of course, to write a story, you only need a pencil, some paper and your imagination. But creating a multimedia story requires a few more tools. For example, if you want video or photographs, you’ll need a digital camera. Or if you want to record some audio, you’ll need special software.

The good news is that most computers, cell phones or tablets – like iPads, for example –have built-in cameras, software and/or apps that allow you to create multimedia pretty easily. There are also a lot of easy-to-use story building tools out there for you to learn and experiment with.

Apps

Audio Memos

Audio Memos

Audio Memos is a professionally made audio recorder. It is very easy to use for interviews, music sessions, and recording ambient sound.

Puppet Pals

Puppet Pals

Create your own unique videos with animation and audio in real time! Simply pick out your actors and backdrops, drag them on to the stage, and tap record. Your movements and audio are recorded in real time for playback later. You can even load your own illustrations into the app!

iMovie

iMovie

iMovie lets you edit your video clips right on your iPad or iPhone. It’s fast, fun, and easy to use! The iMovie app also includes themes with matching titles, transitions, and music.

Software

iBooks Author

iBook Author

Available free on the Mac App Store, iBooks Author is an amazing app that allows anyone to create beautiful interactive books for iPads and Macs. It even has a simple tool for making interactive graphics using your own illustrations.

iMovie

iMovie

It’s never been easier to make it in the movies. Just choose the clips you want to use. Insert titles, add effects, and create a full soundtrack with powerful tools that are as easy as drag and drop.

Video Editor

Video Editor

If you have a Microsoft computer, you can use Wondershare Video Editor to edit your video clips. Just like iMovie, you can edit video clips and add special effects, titles, and cool transitions.
