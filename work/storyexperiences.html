Story Experiences

Not every story can be told with paragraph after paragraph.

Stories are inherently experiences. The way people write or speak can fall flat or lead consumers through a journey.

Designing for stories is no different.

Working with Vox Product, I have done everything from enhancing written pieces to creating entirely different story forms in tandem with editorial teams.

Some of these pieces have required simple illustration.

Other times, pieces are more in depth, requiring alternative storytelling forms of different methods of presenting information.

For instance, this piece I worked on for vox.com with Uy Tieu and Yuri Victor required an alternative to a photo gallery that would allow for a more fluid photo-based form.

I also work on pieces that are solely interactive with few traditional requirements but still have crazy reach. This is a piece I worked on with Josh Laincz for The Verge. It was meant to be simple and highly shareable.

And then some piece combine a little bit of everything.

This piece I worked on with Aidan Feay combines a graphic with enhanced typography, an animated illustration and text to create a clearer and captivating user experience.

That's just a taste of the ways I work to make stories more engaging and engrossing. Making sure content and users are the top priority, I've worked on many more projects than just these.
